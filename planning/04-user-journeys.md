# User Journeys

## Overview

CaseCompass AI is designed around one core principle:

**Users should never feel lost.**

Every interaction should move users from uncertainty to understanding by breaking complex legal research into simple, manageable steps. Rather than overwhelming users with legal terminology or search results, the platform guides them through a structured journey that results in a personalized legal research roadmap.

The following user journeys describe how different users interact with the platform from their initial question to achieving meaningful educational outcomes.

---

# Primary User Journey

## Incarcerated Individual

### Goal

Understand where to begin researching a legal issue without receiving legal advice.

---

## Stage 1 — Initial Question

### User Mindset

The user has a legal concern but does not know what information is relevant.

Examples include:

- Can I appeal my conviction?
- Why was I sentenced this long?
- Did my lawyer make a mistake?
- What happens after a PCR?
- What does this court opinion mean?

### User Needs

- Reassurance
- Simple language
- Clear direction
- Confidence

---

## Stage 2 — Landing Page

The user arrives at CaseCompass AI.

They immediately understand:

- This is an educational legal research tool.
- It does not provide legal advice.
- It helps users understand where to begin.

Primary CTA:

> **Build My Research Roadmap**

Success Criteria

- User understands the purpose of the application.
- User feels encouraged to continue.

---

## Stage 3 — Guided Intake

The user begins answering plain-language questions.

Examples:

- What state was your case in?
- What were you convicted of?
- Have you already appealed?
- What is your biggest question today?
- What documents do you already have?

The intake behaves like a conversation instead of a legal form.

Progress indicators show completion throughout.

Success Criteria

- User completes intake without confusion.
- AI gathers sufficient context.

---

## Stage 4 — AI Analysis

CaseCompass AI processes the responses.

Instead of giving legal advice, it identifies educational topics including:

- Legal concepts
- Relevant procedures
- Court stages
- Important terminology
- Possible research categories

The user sees a brief loading experience explaining what the AI is doing.

Example:

✔ Understanding your case

✔ Identifying legal concepts

✔ Building your research roadmap

✔ Finding educational resources

---

## Stage 5 — Personalized Research Roadmap

The user receives a structured roadmap instead of a search result.

Example

```text
YOUR LEGAL QUESTION

↓

Understand Your Conviction

↓

Learn Important Legal Terms

↓

Research Relevant Statutes

↓

Review Similar Court Cases

↓

Understand Appeal Procedures

↓

Create Questions for Future Legal Assistance
```

Each section includes:

- Plain-language explanations
- Educational summaries
- Suggested research topics
- Relevant legal concepts
- Official citations when available

Success Criteria

The user knows exactly what to research next.

---

## Stage 6 — Explore

The user explores each roadmap section.

Examples

Understand This Legal Term

↓

Read Plain Language Explanation

↓

See Related Cases

↓

Learn Why It Matters

↓

Save Notes

↓

Continue

Navigation always encourages the next logical step.

---

## Stage 7 — AI Guidance

At any point the user may ask questions.

Example

"What is ineffective assistance of counsel?"

The AI responds with:

- Plain-language explanation
- Educational context
- Relevant legal terminology
- Suggested follow-up research
- Related roadmap sections

If appropriate, the AI recommends discussing questions with a licensed attorney.

---

## Stage 8 — Continue Research

The user saves:

- Notes
- Bookmarks
- Roadmaps
- Cases
- Statutes
- Definitions

Returning users resume exactly where they left off.

---

## End State

The user leaves with:

- Greater confidence
- Better understanding
- Organized research
- Reduced confusion
- A clear next step

# Design Principles

Every screen should answer one question:

**What should the user do next?**

Users should never feel stuck or overwhelmed.

Every page should include:

- A clear purpose
- One primary action
- Plain-language guidance
- Visible progress
- Helpful explanations
- Easy navigation

---

# Accessibility Journey

Because many users may have limited literacy or cognitive overload, every journey should include:

- Simple language
- Short paragraphs
- Step-by-step instructions
- Large readable typography
- High color contrast
- Progress indicators
- Visual roadmap cues
- Mobile responsiveness
- Screen reader compatibility
- Keyboard accessibility

---

# Failure States

If the AI cannot confidently answer a question:

The platform should:

- Explain why.
- Identify missing information.
- Suggest official resources.
- Recommend consulting a licensed attorney when appropriate.

The AI should never fabricate legal information or present uncertain information as fact.

---

# Success Metrics

A successful journey results in users who can confidently say:

- "I know where to start."
- "I understand this legal term."
- "I know what to research next."
- "I finally understand what this court opinion means."
- "I feel prepared to continue learning."

---

# Journey Philosophy

CaseCompass AI is not about finding answers—it is about building understanding.

Every interaction should move users from confusion to confidence by providing structure, education, and a clear path forward.

---

# User Journey Summary

> Every user begins with uncertainty and a question. CaseCompass AI transforms that uncertainty into a personalized legal research roadmap, guiding users through complex legal information one step at a time. Rather than replacing legal professionals, the platform empowers individuals with the knowledge and confidence to become informed participants in their own legal research.
