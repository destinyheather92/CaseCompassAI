# Problem Statement

## Overview

Access to legal information does not guarantee access to justice.

Across the United States, millions of incarcerated individuals have access to prison law libraries, statutes, court opinions, and legal reference materials. However, these resources are often written for attorneys, judges, and legal professionals—not for individuals with limited legal education or literacy. As a result, many people are left with access to information but without the knowledge or confidence to use it effectively.

CaseCompass AI was created to bridge that gap.

---

# The Problem

For many incarcerated individuals, the first challenge is not conducting legal research—it's knowing where to begin.

People often enter the legal research process with deeply personal questions:

- Can I appeal my conviction?
- Did my attorney make a mistake?
- Was my sentence calculated correctly?
- What does this court opinion mean?
- Is there anything I can still do?

Unfortunately, traditional legal research tools assume users already understand legal terminology, court procedures, citation formats, and legal strategy. Instead of providing guidance, these systems return overwhelming amounts of information with little context about what is relevant or why it matters.

Without a clear starting point, users frequently become frustrated, discouraged, or spend valuable time researching topics that have little connection to their circumstances.

---

# Why This Matters

The justice system depends on individuals being able to understand legal information that directly affects their lives. Yet many incarcerated individuals face significant barriers that make meaningful legal research difficult, including:

- Limited legal literacy
- Limited access to legal representation
- Reading comprehension challenges
- Complex legal terminology
- Thousands of pages of statutes and case law
- Limited time and resources within correctional facilities
- Lack of guidance on how legal concepts connect to their own case

While prison law libraries provide access to legal materials, they rarely provide the educational support needed to navigate those materials effectively.

The result is a gap between **access to information** and **understanding that information**.

---

# Existing Solutions Fall Short

Most legal research platforms were designed for legal professionals.

They assume users already know:

- Which legal issue applies to their case.
- What statutes to search.
- Which cases are relevant.
- How to read appellate opinions.
- How legal citations work.
- Which procedural rules apply.

For someone with no legal background, these assumptions create an overwhelming experience that often discourages continued research.

Even modern AI tools introduce new risks. They may generate convincing but inaccurate legal information, fail to explain their reasoning, or unintentionally provide legal advice without appropriate safeguards.

---

# Our Opportunity

CaseCompass AI approaches legal research differently.

Instead of acting as another legal search engine, the platform serves as an educational guide that helps users understand what to research before they begin searching.

Through guided conversations written in plain language, CaseCompass AI identifies the user's situation and generates a personalized legal research roadmap that explains:

- What legal concepts may be relevant
- Why those concepts matter
- Which statutes and cases to explore
- What unfamiliar legal terms mean
- Logical next steps for continued research

Rather than replacing attorneys or offering legal opinions, the platform empowers users to become more informed and confident researchers.

---

# Who We Are Solving This For

The primary audience includes incarcerated individuals who:

- Cannot afford an attorney
- Have limited legal literacy
- Need help understanding legal documents
- Feel overwhelmed by legal research
- Have access to legal resources but lack direction

Secondary audiences include:

- Prison education programs
- Correctional facilities
- Legal aid organizations
- Innocence projects
- Law school clinics
- Family members supporting incarcerated loved ones

---

# Why AI

Artificial intelligence has the potential to simplify complex information, identify relevant educational resources, and personalize learning experiences.

When used responsibly, AI can reduce barriers to understanding by translating legal language into plain English, organizing information into logical research paths, and helping users connect unfamiliar legal concepts to their own circumstances.

However, AI should never replace professional legal judgment.

CaseCompass AI is intentionally designed as an educational tool—not a legal advisor. Every recommendation emphasizes learning, transparency, and responsible use while clearly communicating the limitations of the platform.

---

# Success Criteria

CaseCompass AI succeeds when a user:

- Understands their legal issue more clearly.
- Knows what to research next.
- Learns unfamiliar legal terminology.
- Leaves with a personalized research roadmap.
- Feels more confident navigating legal information.
- Can have more informed conversations with attorneys or legal aid organizations.

Success is not measured by legal outcomes, but by increased understanding, confidence, and access to meaningful legal education.

---

# Product Positioning

CaseCompass AI is not designed to replace attorneys, judges, or legal professionals.

Instead, it fills the educational gap that exists between having access to legal information and knowing how to use it.

The platform transforms overwhelming legal resources into structured, personalized learning experiences that help users move from uncertainty to understanding.

---

# Problem Statement Summary

> Millions of incarcerated individuals have access to legal information but lack the guidance needed to understand it. Existing legal research tools assume legal knowledge that many users do not have, leaving them overwhelmed and unsure where to begin. CaseCompass AI addresses this gap by transforming plain-language questions into personalized legal research roadmaps, empowering users to understand legal concepts, navigate complex information, and conduct meaningful legal research with confidence—without providing legal advice or replacing licensed attorneys.
