# UI/UX Principles

# Overview

The user experience of CaseCompass AI is built around a single belief:

> **People should never feel lost while trying to understand the law.**

Every screen, interaction, animation, and piece of content should reduce cognitive load and help users move one step closer to understanding their legal situation.

Unlike traditional legal software, which prioritizes search and information density, CaseCompass AI prioritizes guidance, education, and clarity.

Our goal is not simply to help users find information—it is to help them understand what they are looking at, why it matters, and what they should do next.

---

# Core Design Philosophy

Every experience should move users from:

Confusion

↓

Understanding

↓

Confidence

↓

Action

The interface should feel like a knowledgeable guide walking beside the user—not a database expecting them to already know the answers.

---

# Guiding Principles

## 1. Guidance Over Search

Traditional legal research begins with a search box.

CaseCompass begins with a conversation.

Users shouldn't need to know legal terminology before they can begin.

Instead of asking:

"What statute are you looking for?"

We ask:

"What are you trying to understand?"

---

## 2. Reduce Cognitive Load

Legal research is already mentally demanding.

The interface should never add unnecessary complexity.

Rules:

- One primary action per screen.
- Minimize distractions.
- Progressive disclosure.
- Use whitespace generously.
- Avoid dense paragraphs.
- Break information into manageable sections.

Every screen should feel approachable.

---

## 3. Plain Language First

Everything should be written for people—not lawyers.

Whenever possible:

- Explain before defining.
- Translate before quoting.
- Summarize before expanding.

Target reading level:

Approximately 6th grade.

Complex legal terminology should always include a plain-language explanation.

---

## 4. Show Progress

Users should always know:

- Where they are
- What they've completed
- What comes next

Examples:

- Progress bars
- Roadmap indicators
- Checklists
- Breadcrumbs
- Completed milestones

Progress creates confidence.

---

## 5. One Clear Next Step

Every screen should answer:

> **What should I do next?**

Primary actions should always be obvious.

Avoid presenting multiple competing calls to action.

---

## 6. Teach Before You Ask

Never assume legal knowledge.

If the application asks about:

- Appeals
- PCR
- Habeas Corpus
- Sentencing
- Jurisdiction

It should explain those concepts before requiring a response.

---

## 7. Build Trust Through Transparency

AI should never feel like a black box.

Whenever possible explain:

- Why something was recommended.
- Where information came from.
- Confidence level.
- Educational limitations.

If uncertainty exists, say so.

Trust is built through honesty.

# Personalized Research Roadmaps

The roadmap is the heart of the product.

Every roadmap should feel like progress rather than documentation.

Example

```
Your Question

↓

Understand the Legal Issue

↓

Learn Important Terms

↓

Research Relevant Statutes

↓

Review Related Cases

↓

Build Questions

↓

Continue Learning
```

Users should always know where they are within their roadmap.

---

# AI Interaction Principles

The AI should:

Ask clarifying questions.

Explain legal concepts.

Recommend educational resources.

Suggest next research topics.

Encourage critical thinking.

The AI should never:

Provide legal advice.

Predict legal outcomes.

Guarantee success.

Replace attorneys.

Draft legal strategies.

---

# Error Prevention

Whenever possible:

Prevent mistakes instead of correcting them.

Examples:

Explain unfamiliar questions.

Validate inputs.

Confirm important actions.

Warn before deleting information.

Auto-save progress.

---

# Error Recovery

When errors occur:

Explain:

What happened.

Why it happened.

What the user can do next.

Never blame the user.

Example:

"I couldn't generate your roadmap because I need more information about your case."

Offer:

Retry

Ask Follow-Up Questions

Return to Previous Step

---

# Feedback

Users should always receive immediate feedback.

Examples:

Roadmap Saved

Question Answered

Progress Updated

Definition Saved

Bookmark Added

Loading states should explain what is happening.

Example

✓ Understanding your case

✓ Identifying legal concepts

✓ Building your roadmap

✓ Finding educational resources

# Mobile-First Experience

Many users may primarily access the application from mobile devices.

The mobile experience should never feel like a reduced version.

Prioritize:

Vertical scrolling

Large tap targets

Readable typography

Simplified navigation

Sticky primary actions

Roadmaps optimized for small screens

Best practices:

One question at a time.

Progress indicator.

Plain-language labels.

Helpful examples.

Real-time validation.

Auto-save.

Shake

# UX Success Metrics

The experience is successful when users can confidently answer:

- What am I trying to learn?
- Why does this matter?
- What should I research next?
- Where do I continue?
- How far have I progressed?

If users can answer those five questions, the interface is working.

# Product Experience Statement

CaseCompass AI should feel less like a legal database and more like a trusted guide.

Every interaction should replace uncertainty with direction, transforming overwhelming legal information into a clear, personalized path forward.

Our goal is not to make users feel smarter than lawyers.

Our goal is to make them feel confident enough to continue learning.

---

# UI/UX Mission

> **Every click should reduce confusion. Every screen should build confidence. Every interaction should help users understand where to begin.**
